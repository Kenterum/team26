Preparation to Hour of Code Week

To guarantee that our pupils have a successful day, we planned ahead of time: 1.Read through the educator's guide for Hour of Code. 2. Using Hour of Code to register our event. Every school that registers receives acknowledgement, and registration allows Code.org to track effect. 3.Choosing 2–3 activities/tutorials for the full week and putting them to the test initially. This stage prepared us for assisting students in making connections to past classes, understanding how to encourage learning, and anticipating where some of them could get stuck, in our opinion. 4.Adapted any of Code.org's classes or activities to our class's needs. We're attempting to convince kids that the Hour of Code may be the beginning of their coding and programming adventure, and that they should concentrate on some of the core ideas that are essential to coding in any programming language. We will teach pupils the fundamentals of input, output, loops, and functions.
